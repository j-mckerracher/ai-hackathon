FAQ

Do I need to be a cybersecurity expert to compete?
Not at all. While security knowledge helps, the challenges are rooted in ML research. If you understand machine learning and want to learn how it can be attacked and manipulated, you're exactly who we're looking for.
Can I compete solo, or is a team required?
You can register and compete as a solo competitor or as a team of up to three people. Teams are not required, but given the challenge scope, collaborative skills often provide an edge.
What does the virtual round look like?
The virtual round is hosted on Kaggle. You'll have 24–48 hours to complete the challenges, make submissions, and climb the live leaderboard. Details on dataset access and submission format will be provided to registered teams before the event.
How many teams make it to the in-person final?
A limited number of top-performing teams from the virtual round will be selected as finalists and invited to Purdue University. Finalist count and selection criteria will be announced at launch.
Will there be support or mentorship during the event?
Yes. Registered competitors will have access to challenge-specific documentation and async support during the virtual round. Additional mentorship opportunities will be available to finalists at the in-person event.
What happens at the award gala?
Following the final 24-hour sprint and team presentations to industry judges, BREACH closes with a formal award ceremony and gala recognizing the top performers and celebrating the event's contributors and sponsors.
