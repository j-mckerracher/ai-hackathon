Event Structure

Two Rounds.
One Winner.

BREACH is not your average hackathon. This is an elite competition designed for students who want to go beyond theoretical security — and actually break things.

The event runs in two distinct phases: a grueling virtual qualifier held on Kaggle, and an intense in-person final at Purdue University. Only the sharpest teams advance.

You'll attack, probe, and exploit real AI models across three structured challenges, each targeting a different class of vulnerability in modern machine learning systems. This is adversarial AI research — competitive, timed, and judged by people building the models you're attacking.

01
Phase I · Summer
Virtual Qualifying Round on Kaggle
Open to all registered teams. A 24–48 hour sprint hosted on Kaggle where you'll attack AI systems, submit solutions, and compete on a live leaderboard. Work from anywhere in the world — your score decides your fate.
24–48 hrs Kaggle Platform Live Leaderboard
02
Phase II · Late Aug – Early Sep
In-Person Final at Purdue University
Finalist teams are invited to campus for a 24-hour in-person battle. No safety net, no remote fallback. You'll present your attacks, defend your methodology, and compete for the top prize in front of judges from the industry's most powerful AI labs.
24 hrs West Lafayette, IN Finalist Teams Only
★
Finals Evening
Presentations + Grand Award Gala
Finalist teams present their solutions to a panel of industry judges from Google, Nvidia, Anthropic, and OpenAI. The event concludes with a formal award gala celebrating the best adversarial minds of the year.

The Challenges

Pick Your
Vector.

Three challenges. Three attack surfaces. All competitors attempt every challenge during the virtual round — your aggregate performance determines your ranking.
01
Challenge 01 · Active
Physical Backdoor Attacks in Federated Learning
Your target: an image classification model trained via federated learning on the CelebA dataset. Your weapon: physical objects in the real world. Sunglasses, tattoos, earrings, accessories — you'll design stealthy backdoor triggers embedded in real, plausible physical appearances.
The objective is to poison the model so that your chosen trigger causes consistent misclassification of targeted images — while keeping the model operating normally on clean inputs. The stealthier and more effective your attack, the higher your score.
Maximize Attack Success Rate Maintain Clean Accuracy CelebA Dataset Federated Learning

Who Should Enter

BREACH is open to university students with a serious interest in machine learning security, adversarial AI, or computer security research. No prior competition experience required — only the hunger to find weaknesses that others miss.

  • Teams of 1 to 3 students
  • Current enrollment at an accredited university required
  • All majors and disciplines welcome
  • Competitors must be available for the virtual round dates (summer)
  • Finalists must be available for the in-person event at Purdue (late Aug – early Sep)
  • No prior hackathon experience required

What You Get

Access to curated datasets, structured challenge frameworks, mentorship opportunities during the virtual round, and — for finalists — travel support details announced at selection. Beyond prizes, this is a direct line to the people hiring the next generation of AI security researchers.

Kaggle Platform Note

The virtual round is hosted on Kaggle. Ensure all team members have (or create) Kaggle accounts. Submission details will be sent after registration closes.

Questions

Intel
Briefing.

Do I need to be a cybersecurity expert to compete?
Not at all. While security knowledge helps, the challenges are rooted in ML research. If you understand machine learning and want to learn how it can be attacked and manipulated, you're exactly who we're looking for.
Can I compete solo, or is a team required?
You can register and compete as a solo competitor or as a team of up to three people. Teams are not required, but given the challenge scope, collaborative skills often provide an edge.
What does the virtual round look like?
The virtual round is hosted on Kaggle. You'll have 24–48 hours to complete the challenges, make submissions, and climb the live leaderboard. Details on dataset access and submission format will be provided to registered teams before the event.
How many teams make it to the in-person final?
A limited number of top-performing teams from the virtual round will be selected as finalists and invited to Purdue University. Finalist count and selection criteria will be announced at launch.
Will there be support or mentorship during the event?
Yes. Registered competitors will have access to challenge-specific documentation and async support during the virtual round. Additional mentorship opportunities will be available to finalists at the in-person event.
What happens at the award gala?
Following the final 24-hour sprint and team presentations to industry judges, BREACH closes with a formal award ceremony and gala recognizing the top performers and celebrating the event's contributors and sponsors.
